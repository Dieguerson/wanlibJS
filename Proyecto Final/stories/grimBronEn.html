Grimund Bronzeheart

A small tale of desperation, destruction and the search for justice.

Race Class
Dwarf Warlock
Background Alignment
Guild Artisan Lawful-Good

Grimund Bronzeheart: a dwarf, way too pale to be one, but one still. At 1,4m and 122kg he’s big and heavy, even by dwarven standards. His nose long, his hair white and his beard fiery red. He covers his eyes as best as his hood allows him, they’re yellow, reddish perhaps.

Heavily, our hero walks into an inn. This day has been long and he needs rest, but first, ale. He needs ale. Tired, he lets his body fall on a stool at a table.

- Ale! I need ale, lass! -- He shouts to the bar wench.

- Right away luv!

With his drink comes a question.

- How ‘bout some soup, traveler?

He nods

- Thanks, girl.

4 silver pieces lighter, Grimund finds some solace, and much needed warmth.

After eating and drinking, he pulls out a golden locket, with a red stone embedded on its lid; and a set of jeweler’s tools. As he’s working on his little piece of treasure, a strange, hooded figure sits next to him and says:

- Haven’t seen you here before. Peculiar mark, how did you get it?

The mark is a Fanged-Skull-like burn taking almost his entire right forearm. Grimund, quick to cover it, grunts:

- None of your business, mate.

- Ah, but maybe it is my business, maybe I‘m just curious. Maybe, you want to tell your story to someone.

For some weird reason the dwarf couldn’t refuse the stranger, and started retelling his past misadventures.

- This happened 100 years ago. -- He said.

- I was still a young dwarven blacksmith.

So it was, at the age of 50, just a child by dwarf standards, Grimund started his blacksmith training. By age 64 he had his own shop, and a family, he was happy… he isn’t anymore.

During that year, an orcish invasion raided Dhalborim, it took everyone by surprise, the guards couldn’t do anything to stop it. Of course they weren’t alone. They might have been the best trained individuals in the city, but they weren’t alone. Most dwarves get combat training, so most dwarves fought the invasion.

Little did it help, no matter how many orcs they felled, more kept on coming. They first took the gates, followed by the outer rims of the city. The advance didn’t stop until they reached the keep. In there, dwarves not fit for battle, children, merchants and tourists, were hiding. Grimund’s family was there.

Desperation took hold of the dwarf, there was nothing he could do to save them. So he prayed. He prayed to anyone who would listen: old gods, new gods, demons. It didn’t matter, he needed help, he wanted the power to save his people.

Bronzeheart got lucky, something did listen. Everything went dark, and in his mind, they spoke:

- We are Legion, for we are many.

A sword, fixed in the ground

- Our master, Abaddon, the Destroyer of Life; through us, will grant you what you seek. If power is your desire, we’re ready to bargain.

- I need it! Whatever the cost! My family, my clan, my city; they need me!

- Take us from our resting place then. In exchange, The Destroyer only asks that you bring destruction upon your fickle world.

As he lifted the sword, the weapon changed forms, turning into a gilded Warhammer.

And so, Grimund found himself, and a small group of dwarf warriors surrounded by orcs, at the gates of the keep. A blinding rage took the dwarf’s reason. Hammer in hand he charged. Some orcs died under the mighty hammer, some simply vanished from existence after getting hit. No matter how many of them Grimund killed, orcs kept coming.

- You promised me power in exchange for destruction! Give me the power to destroy then! Fulfill your part of the bargain!

As Bronzeheart shouted this, black fire caught the hammers head, the hilt next, and finally Grimund. This fire did not burn, not him at least. Wherever he placed his sight, he could instantly travel, dead hands would come out of the ground and walls to seize whatever passed near. Simply by shouting at some orcs made them fall, some would later rise from the dead to fight their kinfolk. A few hours later, the dwarf had either burnt, killed or simply made the orcs disappear… and a big part of the city with them.

After the horde was gone, the hammer vanished in thin air and the black fire left Grimund, except for a patch in his right forearm, here it burned him, and it did so deeply, leaving the mark. After the fire died, the dwarf fell to his knees and the voices spoke once more:

- Well done, our pet. We’ll take this as the initial payment, as per our agreement.

Then, Bronzeheart blacked out.

A week later, Grimund wakes up, he’s home. His family’s there with the dwarven high council. They hail him a hero.

After a walk around the city, and talking to some of the survivors, he realizes his actions caused more than half of the city’s destruction, and a good number of dwarf casualties. Still, everyone seems to think of him as a hero. Orcsbane they call him. He hates it. He thinks it should be Dwarvesbane.

Another week passes, and Grimund can’t take it anymore, he’s the cause of all that pain and suffering, he’s dangerous. He leaves his family, his clan, and his city behind. He tells himself it’s for the best, he’d hurt them if he stayed.

The dwarf sets on a quest to get rid of his newly acquired curse. In his travels he learns a thing or two about Legion, Abbadon, and their cults.

He’s made his mind, he’ll use these destructive powers for good, he’ll help anyone that needs it and fight against evil, until he finds a way to lift the curse, or better still, vanish the Destroyer.

His powers are nowhere what they were that night, he can no longer materialize a weapon, he can’t travel instantly, or make enemies disappear anymore, but slowly he learns to control whatever power he has left.

This is the story of his mark and how he got to the inn, as he told it to the hooded figure.

After he finishes his story, the cloaked man speaks:

- Our Lords are satisfied by your deeds so far, dwarf. They’re confident you’ll destroy the world before you find them. However the cult of the Great Legion isn’t as happy, and we’ll make sure you don’t become a hazard, Brother.

The cultist then stands up, making a mark similar to Bronzeheart’s, visible under his left ear and touches Grimund’s, making it burn unbearably.

Our hero wakes up on a bed, at the inn. It was all a dream, he’s had worst and makes none of it. It’s time to leave and continue the righteous search.

In the town’s market, a man with the Fanged-Skull mark under his left ear is buying jade and carving tools.
